## 1. Organizational Structure

### The Cooperative Model (Bottom-Up Ownership)

Raiffeisen is **not** a top-down corporation. The 212 legally independent Raiffeisenbanken (as of end-2024) are each autonomous cooperatives that collectively **own 100% of Raiffeisen Schweiz** -- the central organization headquartered in St. Gallen. This is the inverse of a typical holding structure.

**Three-tier hierarchy:**

| Layer | Entity | Role |
|-------|--------|------|
| **Top (central)** | Raiffeisen Schweiz (Genossenschaft) | Group strategy, risk management, treasury, IT, product development, compliance |
| **Middle** | 21 Regionalverbände (regional associations, organized as Vereine) | Coordination, regional marketing, training, liaison between center and local banks |
| **Bottom (local)** | 212 Raiffeisenbanken | Autonomous client-facing operations, lending, advisory, local governance by elected boards |

**Drivers of consolidation:**
- Rising regulatory burden (FINMA requirements scale poorly for small banks)
- Digitalization costs that require scale to absorb
- Margin pressure on interest income
- Raiffeisen Schweiz actively setting minimum size targets

## 3. Kundenberater Roles & Career Path

### Four Advisory Tiers

| Role (German) | English | Segment | Key Responsibilities |
|---------------|---------|---------|---------------------|
| **Kundenberater/in Basis (KB)** | Basic Customer Advisor | Private clients (shared portfolio) | Accounts, cards, e-banking, basic savings, simple investment solutions |
| **Privatkundenberater/in Individual (PKI)** | Individual Private Client Advisor | Private clients (own portfolio) | Mortgages, home financing, affordability, wealth planning, retirement |
| **Vermögensberater/in (VB)** | Wealth Advisor | Affluent private clients | Investment strategy, portfolio management, estate planning |
| **Firmenkundenberater/in (FK)** | Corporate Client Advisor | SMEs & corporates | Business financing, leasing, FX, succession planning, liquidity |

### Career Progression

Typical path: **KB Basis --> PKI Individual --> Vermögensberater** (specialization track) or **--> Teamleiter/Abteilungsleiter** (management track).

Alternative specializations from PKI: mortgage specialist, retirement specialist, digital specialist.

Corporate track: **FK Trainee --> FK Berater --> Senior FK / Leiter Firmenkunden**

### SAQ Certification (Mandatory under FIDLEG)

Under Article 28 of FinSA (FIDLEG), all client advisors at Swiss financial institutions **must** be entered in a client adviser register. The SAQ (Swiss Association for Quality) certification is the industry standard:

- **Format:** Written + oral examination
- **Validity:** 3 years, then recertification required
- **Standard:** ISO 17024 accredited
- **Scope:** Covers expertise and rules of conduct for the advisory profession
- **Raiffeisen role:** Represented on the SAQ "Client Advisors Bank" Standards Committee
- **In Neustart program:** SAQ cert is the culmination for PKB and PKI roles; voluntary but recommended for FK

## 7. Challenges & Pain Points

### 1. Interest Margin Compression
Net interest income fell 7.5% in 2024. With SNB rate cuts, the spread on mortgages (Raiffeisen's bread and butter at CHF 220B+) is compressing. Brenna acknowledged that 2025-2026 will see "schwierigeres Zinsumfeld" (more difficult interest rate environment).

### 2. Revenue Concentration Risk
Raiffeisen is overwhelmingly a mortgage bank. Non-interest income is only 27.4% of total revenue -- far below UBS or even cantonal banks. Brenna's #1 strategic priority is diversification into investment/wealth management.

### 3. Digital Execution Failures
The CHF 47M super-app write-off is a significant credibility issue. The digital mortgage project was also delayed. Raiffeisen's digital capabilities lag competitors. Inside Paradeplatz reported the true cost may be even higher than the CHF 47M disclosed.

### 4. Consolidation Pressure on Local Banks
Small Raiffeisenbanken face existential pressure: rising regulatory costs, IT modernization demands, and Raiffeisen Schweiz's minimum size benchmarks (20 FTEs, CHF 650M balance sheet). Mergers are accelerating -- the network shrank from ~243 to ~212 banks in just one year.

### 5. Neobank Competition
Free accounts from Yuh (PostFinance/Swissquote), Neon, Revolut, etc. put pressure on traditional fee structures. Average bank account fees in Switzerland: CHF 30/year at traditional banks vs. CHF 0 at neobanks. Raiffeisen's value proposition of "local proximity" is tested as younger customers go digital-first.

### 6. Leadership Instability
Three GL members departing in 18 months (Huber, Reist, plus VR chair Mueller). Gabriel Brenna is still in his first months. Strategy is in a holding pattern (2025 strategy extended, new strategy not yet defined).

### 7. Talent & Training Burden
The "Neustart" program exists because traditional banking talent pipelines are insufficient. SAQ recertification every 3 years adds ongoing cost. The shift from product selling to holistic advisory requires significant retraining of ~10,000+ employees.

### 8. Branch Cost Structure
Despite 20%+ branch reductions over a decade, Raiffeisen still operates 761+ locations -- the densest network in Switzerland. Each branch has fixed costs (rent, staff, compliance) that are increasingly hard to justify as transaction volumes move digital.

## 9. Mortgage Customer Journey

### Overview: Raiffeisen's Core Business

Raiffeisen is Switzerland's **third-largest mortgage lender** with an 18.4% market share (2025), behind the Cantonal Banks (~38%) and UBS. Mortgage volume reached **CHF 230.9 billion** in 2025 (+4.6% YoY). This is the bank's bread and butter -- mortgages represent the vast majority of revenue.

### The Mortgage Journey: Step by Step

#### Phase 1: Research & Self-Assessment (Online)

Raiffeisen provides several **online calculators** at raiffeisen.ch:

| Tool | What It Does |
|------|-------------|
| **Hypothekenrechner** | Calculates required equity, affordable purchase price, and suggests financing models |
| **Tragbarkeitsrechner** | Tests whether gross income supports the mortgage (1/3 rule) |
| **Mieten-oder-Kaufen-Rechner** | Compares renting vs. buying financially |
| **Steuerrechner** | Estimates homeowner tax benefits |
| **Zinsalarm** | Set interest rate alerts in E-Banking, checked daily |

The ROPO effect (Research Online, Purchase Offline) is strong: ~50% of customers research online before contacting the bank.

#### Phase 2: Online Mortgage Application (Digital Path)

Since ~2024, Raiffeisen offers an **end-to-end online mortgage process** (delayed from late 2025 originally):

1. **Property details** -- enter information about desired property + planned renovations
2. **Property valuation** -- bank assesses the purchase price
3. **Financial profile** -- personal details, income, expenses, assets
4. **Personalized recommendation** -- optimal equity deployment + recommended mortgage mix
5. **Online signing** -- mortgage can be concluded digitally

A virtual advisor guides the customer with individual recommendations through the process.

#### Phase 3: In-Branch Advisory (Hybrid Path)

Most mortgage customers still go through personal advisory, especially for first-time buyers:

1. **Appointment scheduling** -- online via raiffeisen.ch or by phone
2. **Document preparation** -- customer gathers required documents (see checklist below)
3. **Needs analysis** -- advisor assesses financial situation, goals, risk tolerance
4. **Financing proposal** -- tailored mortgage mix (Festhypothek / SARON / Variable)
5. **Tragbarkeit check** -- formal affordability assessment using kalkulatorischer Zins
6. **Offer & negotiation** -- terms, rates, discounts
7. **Signing** -- in branch or digitally
8. **Disbursement** -- funds released at property transfer

Advisory can happen **in-person at the branch** or **via video conference**, depending on complexity and customer preference.

#### Phase 4: Ongoing Relationship

- **Hypothek erneuern** -- renewal advisory before maturity
- **Amortisation tracking** -- 2. Hypothek must be repaid within 15 years or by retirement
- **Cross-sell triggers** -- mortgage events trigger Vorsorge, insurance, investment conversations

### Required Documents (Checkliste)

**Personal / Financial:**
- Current salary statement (incl. bonus history for last 3 years)
- Recent tax return and account statements
- Pension fund certificate (2. Säule)
- Pillar 3a statements
- Life/disability insurance policies
- Debt registry extract (Betreibungsregisterauszug)
- Leasing and credit agreements

**Property:**
- Land registry extract (Grundbuchauszug)
- Official property valuation (amtliche Schätzung)
- Purchase contract or draft
- Building plans and energy certificate (GEAK)
- Property insurance documentation
- Interior/exterior photos
- Rental agreements (if investment property)

**For new construction/renovation:** Building permits, contractor agreements, cost estimates, construction insurance

**For condominiums:** Usage regulations (Nutzungsreglement), ownership structure, Stockwerkeigentümerreglement

**For self-employed:** Financial statements, tax records, Handelsregister extract

### Key Financing Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Minimum equity** | 20% of purchase price |
| **Of which hard equity** | Min. 10% must be "echte" Eigenmittel (savings, securities, 3a) -- 2. Säule (pension fund) alone is not sufficient |
| **1. Hypothek** | Up to 66% of property value |
| **2. Hypothek** | Remaining amount (up to 80%), must be amortized within 15 years or by retirement |
| **Tragbarkeit** | Total housing costs (mortgage interest + amortization + Nebenkosten) must not exceed 1/3 of gross income |
| **Kalkulatorischer Zins** | Long-term average interest rate (~5%) used for stress test, not current market rate |
| **Nebenkosten** | Typically 1% of property value per year for maintenance/repairs |

### Mortgage Products

| Product | Feature | Best For |
|---------|---------|----------|
| **Festhypothek** | Fixed rate for 2-15 years | Rate security, budget certainty |
| **SARON Flex Hypothek** | Variable rate based on SARON reference | Benefiting from low/falling rates |
| **Variable Hypothek** | Adjustable rate, switch anytime | Maximum flexibility |

### Special Discounts

- **Eco-Rabatt** -- for sustainable construction/renovation (Minergie, etc.)
- **Familien-Rabatt** -- for households with children
- **Neukundenrabatt** -- for new mortgage customers switching to Raiffeisen

## 10. Vorsorge (Retirement Planning) Advisory

### Products & Services

#### Pillar 3a (Tax-Advantaged Retirement Savings)

| Product | Type | Key Feature |
|---------|------|-------------|
| **Vorsorgekonto 3a** | Savings account | Preferred interest rate, tax deductible |
| **Digitale Säule 3a** | Digital account + funds | Open/manage via E-Banking, invest in funds |
| **Vorsorgefonds** | Fund-based 3a | Multiple risk profiles, higher return potential |

Note: Raiffeisen's 3a fund costs are on the expensive side -- TER of 1.0-1.5% with entry commissions up to 2%, making it one of the pricier 3a providers compared to fintech alternatives.

#### Pension Planning (Pensionsberatung)

Comprehensive retirement advisory covering:

- **Income analysis** -- AHV pension calculation, pension fund projections, gap analysis
- **Capital vs. annuity** -- pension fund withdrawal options (Rente vs. Kapital)
- **Asset development** -- projected wealth at retirement and beyond
- **Tax optimization** -- staggered 3a withdrawals, pension fund buy-ins
- **Estate planning** -- inheritance law, Vorsorgeauftrag (advance directive)
- **Early retirement** -- feasibility analysis, bridging strategies

#### Advisory Timeline (Recommended by Raiffeisen)

| Age Range | Advisory Focus |
|-----------|---------------|
| **18-44** | Start 3a contributions, build savings habit |
| **45-49** | Initial retirement vision, gap assessment, optimize contributions |
| **50-59** | Concrete planning: set retirement date, evaluate pension buy-ins, 3a strategy |
| **60-65** | Finalize withdrawal method, schedule benefit disbursements, estate prep |

#### Preparation Checklist for Pensionsberatung

Clients should bring:
- Current spending budget
- Bank/postal account statements
- Securities documentation
- Tax filings (last 2-3 years)
- AHV statement (individuelle Vorausberechnung)
- Pension fund certificate (Vorsorgeausweis)
- Insurance policies (life, disability)
- Property documentation (if homeowner)
- Estate documents (Ehe-/Erbvertrag, Testament)

#### Insurance Products (Cross-Sold with Vorsorge)

| Product | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Todesfall-Versicherung | Death benefit for beneficiaries |
| Erwerbsunfähigkeits-Rente | Disability income protection |
| Kapital-Lebensversicherung | Capital insurance with tax advantages |
| Amortisations-Versicherung | Mortgage debt repayment on death/disability |
| Haushaltsversicherung | Household contents coverage |
| Sparzielabsicherung | Goal protection without tax penalty |

### Cross-Selling: The Mortgage-Vorsorge-Investment Triangle

Raiffeisen's stated strategic shift is from "Hypothekenverkäufer" (mortgage seller) to "Lösungsanbieter" (solution provider). The cross-sell logic:

1. **Mortgage event** → triggers Vorsorge discussion (pension fund withdrawal for equity, 3a for indirect amortization)
2. **Vorsorge advisory** → reveals investment gap (income replacement in retirement) → investment products
3. **Life events** → birth of child (insurance, savings plan), inheritance (wealth management), divorce (pension splitting)
4. **Renewal events** → mortgage renewal every 5-10 years → complete financial review

**CEO Brenna's directive (2026):** "We are today the leading mortgage bank, now we must also become a strong investment bank." Investment in CHF 550M strategy to expand advisory capabilities.

Originally planned for late 2025, **postponed to 2026** for quality reasons. The current online mortgage tool allows application and basic proposal generation, but full end-to-end digital closing is still being rolled out.

### What Requires In-Branch (Cannot Be Done Online)

- Complex mortgage advisory (multi-property, renovation financing)
- Comprehensive Pensionsberatung (pension planning)
- Wealth management mandate setup (high-touch)
- Business account opening (for companies)
- Safe deposit box access
- Notarized document handling

### Key Competitor Profiles

**UBS:** Global scale, strongest brand, best digital tools, but impersonal and expensive. Post-CS merger creates the dominant Swiss bank but with integration distractions.

**Zürcher Kantonalbank (ZKB):** Strongest cantonal bank, excellent digital (Frankly 3a), state guarantee. Main rival in Zurich region. ZKB is where Raiffeisen's Roger Reist is departing to.

**PostFinance:** Ubiquitous through Post network, cheapest traditional option, Yuh neobank partnership. Limited advisory, no own mortgage business (partners with others).

**Neon / Yuh / Revolut:** Free accounts, superior FX rates, mobile-first. Capture younger demographics. Cannot offer mortgages or in-person advisory -- complementary rather than fully substitutive, but erode fee income on transaction banking.

## 15. Salesteq Relevance & Angles

Based on this research, key entry points for a Salesteq engagement:

1. **Advisory productivity gap:** Raiffeisen's strategy explicitly aims to "free advisor time through digitization." AI-powered client preparation, meeting summaries, and next-best-action recommendations directly address this.

2. **Wealth management expansion:** Brenna's top priority. Moving 10,000+ retail bankers toward investment advisory requires massive enablement -- AI coaching, product knowledge, client profiling.

3. **Digital channel weakness:** After the CHF 47M app failure, Raiffeisen needs quick wins in digital. An AI assistant layer could enhance the existing E-Banking app without rebuilding it.

4. **212 semi-autonomous banks = 212 potential buyers:** Each Raiffeisenbank makes independent purchasing decisions within Raiffeisen Schweiz's framework. A pilot at one bank could spread virally.

5. **BSI CRM is only at the KSC (200 users):** The 10,000+ advisors at local banks likely have limited CRM/AI tooling. There is a gap between central capabilities and local bank reality.

6. **Consolidation creates efficiency pressure:** Merged banks need to integrate teams, standardize processes, and do more with fewer people -- exactly where AI operations excel.

7. **Training pipeline as entry:** Integrating AI into the Neustart program or advisor certification prep could be a low-resistance entry point that creates long-term dependency.
